Fascinating Capybara Facts

Social Creatures

Capybaras are highly social animals that live in groups of 10-20 individuals. They're known for their friendly disposition and often allow other animals to sit on them!

Fun fact: Capybaras can recognize and remember individuals in their social groups.

Semi-Aquatic

These amazing creatures are excellent swimmers! They can hold their breath underwater for up to five minutes and often sleep in water with just their noses poking out.

Fun fact: Their partially webbed feet help them navigate through water with ease.

Gentle Giants

Weighing up to 150 pounds, capybaras are the largest rodents in the world. Despite their size, they're known for being gentle and docile.

Fun fact: Their closest relatives are guinea pigs and rock cavies.

Vocal Communicators

Capybaras make various sounds to communicate, including purrs, whistles, clicks, and barks. They're quite the chatterboxes!

Fun fact: Baby capybaras make a unique sound similar to a bird's chirp.

Did You Know?

Capybaras are herbivores and eat mainly grasses and aquatic plants.

Capybaras can live up to 8-10 years in the wild and 12 years in captivity.

Baby capybaras are called pups, and they can swim within hours of being born!
